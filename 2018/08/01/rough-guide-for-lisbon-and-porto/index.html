Rough Guide for Lisbon and Porto

Published by C Zhang on 1st August 2018

Luuukie and Seda:

Here’s my personal recommendation for stuff to do in Lisboa y Porto.

Don’t take this list exclusively, I’ve only listed the places that I feel like you should go, but definitely take it with a pinch of salt (apart from the food sections, I’d say).

There are great many places that because of arbitrary factors I cannot go, e.g., foz do Douro in Porto. So just because I haven’t listed something here doesn’t mean that you shouldn’t go.

Again, take my guide with a pinch of salt.

Have fun.

C.

Lisboa

See

My recommendation is apart from the sights within Lisboa, including but not limited to Castelo de S. Jorge, Praça do Comércio

I’m not into history so I cannot recommend good museums and shit

Might want to try Lxfactory if you’re into artsy stuff1

BUT MOST IMPORTANTLY

  1. City of Belem (including but not limited to Belem Tower, Jerónimos Monastery) to get here get a train from Santos2: Linda de Cascais
  2. Cascais: end of the train line, esp: Boca de Inferno3.

(Belem + Cascais makes a great little day trip).

Other bits-and-bobs:

Lisbon is very hilly so be prepared to embrace on walking-incentive journey. But if you’re into suicidal walking:

Escadinhas de São Crispim 3 – great little place for photography.

And yes take the tram route.

Eat

A Primavera do Jerónimo (portuguesa cuisine, family-run, super nice. Only draw-back: place is a bit crammed and you might want to make reservation/arrive early)4

Ramiro – seafood at alright price (w/ local delicacies)5

Casa Portuguesa do Pastel de Bacalhau6 – fishcakes and whatnot

O Chiado Casual sit-down food.7

Pateis de Nata (HAVE IT HERE DON’T WAIT FOR PORTO BECAUSE IT’S A LISBOA DELICACY):

Pasteis de Belém 8: hands-down best in the world.

Manteigaria9: not bad but I like Belem better. Locals apparently like Manteigaria better, they’re wrong, obs.

Drink

Definitely try their ginjinha (cheer liquor): A Ginjinha10

Delirium Cafe Lisboa (if you like Delirium beer, this is the place to go, pricy)11

I haven’t had much time to drink because I was not in Lisbon by myself but I got the impression that there are quite a lot of wine bars around and some hipster cocktail bar.

Porto

Because of horrible weather I didn’t get to go to many places.

Definitely get some port flowing in your system.

Much walkable than Lisboa.

See

Porto Cathedral12

Luís I Bridge13 (I walked from city centre to Vila Nova de Gaia via here).

Livraria Lello14

Eat

Taberna Santo António15

There are so many restaurants I want to try but I only have one stomach.

Drink

I went here for port tasting: Real Companhia Velha, it’s alright.

Tl;dr and other bits and bobs:

  1. Eat Pateis de Nata until you pass out, drink a lot of coffee too (much better than Spanish shit)
  2. Eat Portuguese food until you pass out.
  3. Drink Vinho Verde (green wine)
